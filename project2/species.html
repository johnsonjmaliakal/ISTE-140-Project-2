Greate White Shark

The great white shark is the biggest of all predatory sharks. The length of that shark is approximately 20 feet and weight about 6000 pounds.

Whale Shark

Whale sharks, most commonly known for being the world’s largest species of fish, are mostly harmless sharks that can reach up to eighteen meters in length. Whale sharks have a unique series of checkerboard marks and white spots across their back that are as unique to each individual as a human fingerprint and filter-feed on krill.

Hammerhead Sharks

Hammerhead sharks are easily identifiable with their iconic hammerhead or shovel shaped heads. These types of sharks have the best visual range due to their wide-set eyes.
